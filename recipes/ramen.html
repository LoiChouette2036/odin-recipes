Descripton

Tonkotsu ramen is a popular Japanese dish that features noodles served in a rich and savory broth made with pork bones, called tonkotsu.
The broth is simmered for many hours to extract the maximum amount of flavor and create a creamy and thick consistency.

Ingredients :

  • Pork bones
  • Water
  • Soy sauce
  • Mirin
  • Sake
  • Salt
  • Sugar
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Scallions
  • Sesame oil
  • Noodles
  • Chashu (braised pork belly)
  • Soft-boiled egg
  • Nori (dried seaweed)

Instructions :

  1. Clean and rinse the pork bones thoroughly.
  2. Place the pork bones in a large pot and cover with water.
  3. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for several hours.
  4. Skim off anu scum that rises to the surface.
  5. Add the aromatics (garlic, ginger, scallions) and continue to simmer for a few more hours.
  6. Remove the bones and strain the broth through a fine-mesh sieve.
  7. Add soy sauce, mirin, sake, salt, sugar, and sesame oil to the broth and stir to combine.
  8. Cook the noodles according to the package instructions.
  9. Slice the chashu into thin pieces.
  10. Soft-boil the egg and slice in half.
  11. Assemble the bowl of ramen by placing the cooked noodles in a bowl, ladling the hot broth over them, and arranging the chashu, egg, and nori on top.

That's a basic overview of how to make tonkotsu ramen. There are many variations and adjustments you can make to suit your taste preferences.
